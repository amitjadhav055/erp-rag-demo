PURCHASE ORDER MANAGEMENT GUIDE
Document ID: PROC-GUIDE-002
Department: Procurement
Last Updated: February 2026

INTRODUCTION
This guide covers the end-to-end purchase order (PO) lifecycle in the ERP system, from creation through to closure. All procurement activities must be conducted through the ERP to maintain audit compliance and spend visibility.

CREATING A PURCHASE ORDER
POs are created in the procurement module by authorised requestors. Required fields: vendor name, vendor ID, delivery address, requested delivery date, line items (description, quantity, unit price, GL account code), and budget approval reference.

PO value thresholds and approval levels:
- Up to ₹50,000: Department Manager approval
- ₹50,001 to ₹5,00,000: Procurement Manager approval
- ₹5,00,001 to ₹25,00,000: Finance Director approval
- Above ₹25,00,000: CEO approval required

PO STATUS CODES
DRAFT: PO has been created but not submitted for approval.
PENDING_APPROVAL: PO submitted and awaiting approver action.
APPROVED: PO approved and transmitted to vendor.
PARTIALLY_RECEIVED: Goods receipt posted for some but not all line items.
FULLY_RECEIVED: All items received and goods receipt completed.
INVOICED: Invoice received and matched against PO.
CLOSED: PO fully processed, payment completed, and closed in system.
CANCELLED: PO cancelled before goods receipt. Requires Finance approval to cancel after receipt.

GOODS RECEIPT PROCESS
When goods arrive, the warehouse team must post a goods receipt (GR) in the ERP within 24 hours of delivery. The GR records: PO number, actual quantities received, delivery date, condition of goods, and warehouse location. Any discrepancy between ordered and received quantities must be noted in the GR comments field. Partial deliveries are allowed — the PO remains open until fully received.

VENDOR MANAGEMENT
All vendors must be registered and approved before a PO can be raised. Vendor registration requires: business registration documents, GST number, bank account details, and at least two trade references. Vendor performance is tracked quarterly based on: on-time delivery rate, invoice accuracy rate, and quality of goods received.

COMMON ERP ERRORS IN PROCUREMENT MODULE
PO-ERR-101: Budget exceeded. The PO value exceeds the available budget for the specified GL account. Action: Request budget amendment from Finance before proceeding.
PO-ERR-102: Vendor on hold. The selected vendor has been placed on payment hold. Action: Contact Accounts Payable to resolve the hold before raising a PO.
PO-ERR-103: Invalid GL account. The specified GL account code does not exist or is inactive. Action: Contact Finance to verify the correct GL code.
PO-ERR-104: Delivery date in the past. Action: Update the delivery date to a future date.
PO-ERR-105: Duplicate PO detected. A PO with the same vendor and line items was recently created. Action: Verify this is not a duplicate before proceeding.

REPORTING AND AUDIT
All PO activity is logged with timestamps and user IDs. Monthly spend reports by vendor, department, and GL account are available in the analytics module. Audit logs are retained for 7 years in accordance with financial record keeping requirements.
