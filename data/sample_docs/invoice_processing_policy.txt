INVOICE PROCESSING POLICY
Document ID: FIN-POL-001
Department: Finance & Procurement
Last Updated: January 2026

OVERVIEW
This document outlines the standard procedures for invoice processing within the ERP system. All invoices received from vendors must go through the automated scanning and verification pipeline before being approved for payment.

INVOICE RECEIPT
All vendor invoices must be submitted to the designated invoice inbox at invoices@company.com. Invoices submitted through any other channel will not be processed. Accepted formats are PDF, TIFF, and scanned image files. Each invoice must include: vendor name, vendor ID, invoice number, invoice date, line items with quantities and unit prices, total amount, and purchase order (PO) reference number.

AUTOMATED SCANNING PIPELINE
Upon receipt, invoices are automatically detected by the email monitoring service and transferred to the OCI Object Storage bucket for processing. The OCR extraction layer then processes the document using a combination of Tesseract, PyMuPDF, and pdfplumber libraries to extract text content. Extracted text is passed through the LLM structuring module which identifies and maps fields to the standard invoice schema. Structured data is validated against expected field types and formats.

THREE-WAY MATCHING REQUIREMENTS
Before any invoice is approved for payment, the system performs three-way matching:
1. Purchase Order Match: The invoice PO number must correspond to an open PO in the ERP system.
2. Goods Receipt Match: The items and quantities on the invoice must match the goods receipt record.
3. Price Verification: Unit prices must not exceed the agreed contract rates by more than 2%.

If all three checks pass, the invoice is automatically approved and a voucher is created in the ERP system. If any check fails, the invoice is flagged and routed to the relevant approver for manual review.

APPROVAL WORKFLOW
Invoices that fail automated matching are routed based on the discrepancy type. Price discrepancies under 5% are routed to the Procurement Manager. Price discrepancies over 5% are escalated to the Finance Director. Missing PO references are routed to the originating department head. Quantity discrepancies are sent to the Warehouse Manager for verification.

PAYMENT TERMS
Standard payment terms are Net-30 from invoice date unless otherwise specified in the vendor contract. Early payment discount terms (e.g., 2/10 Net-30) are automatically detected by the system and flagged for expedited processing. Late payment penalties are tracked in the vendor management module.

ERROR CODES AND RESOLUTION
ERR-001: PO number not found in system. Action: Contact procurement team to verify PO status.
ERR-002: Invoice already processed. Action: Check for duplicate submission. Contact vendor if needed.
ERR-003: Amount exceeds PO value. Action: Requires Finance Director approval before processing.
ERR-004: Vendor ID not recognised. Action: Vendor must be registered in the system before invoice can be processed.
ERR-005: OCR extraction confidence below threshold. Action: Manual data entry required. Contact the finance operations team.
